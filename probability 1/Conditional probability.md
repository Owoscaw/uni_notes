
Given $A, B$ are [[Sample spaces and events#Events|events]], where $\mathbb{P}(B)>0$, we define:
$$\Huge \mathbb{P}(A|B)=\frac{\mathbb{P}(A\cap B)}{\mathbb{P}(B)}$$
For any event $B\subseteq \Omega$, with $\mathbb{P}(B)>0$, $\mathbb{P}(\cdot|B)$ satisfies all [[Probability definition#Axioms|axioms of probability]]. Given a [[Probability definition#Sigma algebra|probability space]], $(\Omega,\mathcal{F},\mathbb{P})$ we define:
$$\Huge \mathbb{P}|_{B}:\mathcal{F}\mapsto\Re\,\text{as follows:}$$
$$\Huge \mathbb{P}|_B(A):=\mathbb{P}(A|B)$$
